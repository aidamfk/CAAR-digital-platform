Home Insurance

Why home insurance is important

Homeowners insurance protects your home, belongings, and finances from damage, theft, or accidents.

What is Homeowners Insurance?

Homeowners insurance is financial protection for your home and belongings against damage caused by events such as fire, storms, theft, or vandalism. You pay a regular premium to an insurance company, and in return, the company pays for repairs or losses if a covered event occurs.

It is one of the most fundamental forms of insurance for individuals and families, providing a financial safety net against the unexpected.

What Does It Cover?

A comprehensive homeowners insurance policy typically covers several areas of risk:

  • The structure of your home — repairs or reconstruction following fire, storms, floods, or other covered disasters
  • Personal belongings — furniture, electronics, clothing, and other personal property damaged or stolen
  • Liability coverage — protection if someone is injured on your property or if you accidentally damage someone else's property
  • Additional living expenses — hotel, rental, and meal costs if you cannot live in your home during repairs after a covered disaster

Natural Disaster Coverage (Cat-Nat)

In Algeria, Natural Disaster Insurance (Cat-Nat) is mandatory by law for all insured properties. This coverage specifically protects against exceptional natural events including:

  • Earthquakes and seismic activity
  • Flooding and inundation
  • Landslides and mudslides
  • Exceptional storms and high winds

CAAR's Cat-Nat coverage ensures that policyholders are protected when natural catastrophes strike — events that would otherwise cause devastating financial losses.

Key Takeaways

  • Home insurance protects your building, contents, and personal liability in one policy
  • Liability coverage protects you if someone is injured on your property
  • Living expense coverage helps if you must temporarily relocate after a disaster
  • Natural Disaster (Cat-Nat) coverage is mandatory in Algeria for all insured properties
  • Most mortgage lenders require active homeowners insurance before completing a purchase

How to Choose the Right Coverage

When selecting a home insurance policy, consider the following:

  • Accurately assess the value of your property and all belongings
  • Understand what risks are most relevant to your location (flood zones, seismic risk areas)
  • Review exclusions carefully — know what is not covered
  • Update your policy after renovations, major purchases, or structural changes
