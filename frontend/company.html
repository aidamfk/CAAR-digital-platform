About CAAR

CAAR is a national insurance and reinsurance company serving individuals, professionals, and institutions.

Company Overview

The Compagnie Algérienne d'Assurance et de Réassurance (CAAR) is a major player in the national insurance market. Founded in 1963, it has supported the country's economic development for decades, providing comprehensive protection for individuals, businesses, and industrial complexes.

Company History

1963

Creation of CAAR Insurance

CAAR was established as Algeria's national insurance and reinsurance company, tasked with protecting the country's economic development.

1988

Transformation into a Joint-Stock Company

CAAR restructured into a joint-stock company, marking a pivotal shift toward a more autonomous and commercially oriented model.

2006

Introduction of New Regulatory Instruments

The company adapted to new national insurance regulations, reinforcing its governance and compliance frameworks.

2006

The Multiple Missions of CAAR Insurance

CAAR expanded its mandate, broadening its portfolio across insurance, reinsurance, and financial investment activities.

2011

Creation of the CAARAMA Subsidiary

CAAR launched CAARAMA, a dedicated subsidiary reinforcing its presence in specialized insurance segments.

Today

Market Leadership Consolidated

CAAR has not only maintained its position as a leader in industrial risk but has also increased its market share, continuing to serve individuals, businesses, and major national projects.
